# Business Associate Agreement (BAA)

**Upstream Healthcare, Inc. Business Associate Agreement**

This Business Associate Agreement ("BAA") is entered into by and between:

**Covered Entity:** [CUSTOMER NAME] ("Covered Entity")

**Business Associate:** Upstream Healthcare, Inc. ("Business Associate")

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

## 1. Definitions

Terms used but not otherwise defined in this BAA shall have the same meaning as those terms in the HIPAA Rules (45 CFR Parts 160, 162, and 164).

**1.1 "Protected Health Information" (PHI)** means individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium.

**1.2 "Electronic Protected Health Information" (ePHI)** means PHI that is transmitted or maintained in electronic media.

**1.3 "HIPAA Rules"** means the Privacy, Security, Breach Notification, and Enforcement Rules at 45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164.

## 2. Permitted Uses and Disclosures

**2.1 Services Provided.** Business Associate may use or disclose PHI solely to perform the following services on behalf of Covered Entity:

- Claims intelligence analysis and processing
- Denial prediction and prevention
- Revenue cycle analytics
- Automated claim scoring and recommendations
- Audit trail and compliance reporting

**2.2 Business Associate's Own Operations.** Business Associate may use PHI for its proper management and administration and to carry out its legal responsibilities.

**2.3 De-identification.** Business Associate may de-identify PHI in accordance with 45 CFR ยง 164.514(a)-(c).

## 3. AI and Machine Learning Specific Provisions

**3.1 Model Training Restrictions.** Business Associate SHALL NOT use Covered Entity's PHI to train machine learning models that will be used for other customers without explicit written consent.

**3.2 Explainability.** Business Associate shall provide, upon request:
- Explanation of AI-driven recommendations
- Feature importance for risk scores
- Audit logs of automated decisions

**3.3 Human Review.** All AI recommendations that result in claim denials or significant financial impact shall be subject to human review before action is taken.

**3.4 Algorithm Updates.** Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity of material changes to AI algorithms that affect PHI processing.

## 4. Data Use Limitations

**4.1 Minimum Necessary.** Business Associate shall request, use, and disclose only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

**4.2 No Sale of PHI.** Business Associate shall not sell PHI.

**4.3 Data Retention.** Business Associate shall retain PHI only for the period necessary to fulfill contractual obligations, subject to legal retention requirements.

**4.4 Return or Destruction.** Upon termination, Business Associate shall return or destroy all PHI received from Covered Entity, if feasible.

## 5. Security Safeguards

**5.1 Administrative Safeguards.** Business Associate shall implement policies and procedures to prevent, detect, contain, and correct security violations.

**5.2 Physical Safeguards.** Business Associate shall implement physical measures to protect electronic systems and data.

**5.3 Technical Safeguards.** Business Associate shall implement:
- AES-256 encryption at rest and in transit
- Access controls and authentication
- Audit logging of all PHI access
- Automatic session timeout

**5.4 Workforce Training.** Business Associate shall train all workforce members on HIPAA requirements.

## 6. Breach Notification

**6.1 Discovery.** Business Associate shall report any Security Incident or Breach of Unsecured PHI to Covered Entity without unreasonable delay, and in no case later than **24 hours** after discovery.

**6.2 Contents.** Breach notifications shall include:
- Description of the incident
- Types of PHI involved
- Individuals affected (if known)
- Steps taken to mitigate harm
- Steps to prevent future occurrences

**6.3 Documentation.** Business Associate shall document all Security Incidents and Breaches and make documentation available to Covered Entity.

## 7. Subcontractors

**7.1 Binding Subcontractors.** Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI agree to the same restrictions and conditions in this BAA.

**7.2 Subcontractor List.** Business Associate shall provide a list of subcontractors with access to PHI upon request.

## 8. Audit Rights

**8.1 Records Access.** Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of HHS.

**8.2 Customer Audit.** Covered Entity may audit Business Associate's compliance with this BAA upon reasonable notice.

## 9. Term and Termination

**9.1 Term.** This BAA shall be effective on the Effective Date and remain in effect until terminated.

**9.2 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this BAA if the other party materially breaches this BAA and fails to cure within 30 days of notice.

**9.3 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Business Associate shall return or destroy all PHI as specified in Section 4.4.

## 10. General Provisions

**10.1 Amendment.** This BAA may be amended only in writing signed by both parties.

**10.2 Interpretation.** Any ambiguity shall be resolved to permit compliance with HIPAA Rules.

**10.3 Survival.** Obligations that by their nature should survive termination shall survive.
